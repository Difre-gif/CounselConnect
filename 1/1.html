Overcoming the Reluctance of MYP 3 Students to Seek Counseling Support

by John Doe

In many schools, including MYP 3, students often face emotional, academic, or social challenges. However, due to a variety of reasons, they may feel reluctant to seek support from school counselors. This reluctance is primarily driven by the fear of judgment, lack of anonymity, or a simple feeling of vulnerability. As a result, many students go through difficulties in silence, which could impact their mental well-being. To address this issue, we propose designing a website that offers anonymous communication between students and counselors. By allowing students to communicate without revealing their identity, the website can help reduce the stigma associated with seeking help. Students can freely express their thoughts and feelings, knowing that their identity will remain confidential. Additionally, incorporating an AI-based Natural Language Processing (NLP) tool can help assess the urgency or seriousness of the student's issue. The tool would analyze the student's messages and determine whether the issue requires the attention of a professional school counselor or if a peer counselor can provide adequate support. This not only streamlines the counseling process but also ensures that students receive appropriate assistance in a timely manner. By ensuring anonymity and confidentiality, the website can eliminate the fear students may have about seeking help. This approach increases their confidence in reaching out for support, which in turn enhances their well-being and academic performance.
